HISTORIC TIMELINE

"Unveiling the Hidden Heroes: Women Shaping Technological History"

First Coding Program (1843)

Ada Lovelace, an English mathematician, is considered the world's first computer programmer. She collaborated with Charles Babbage on his Analytical Engine and wrote an algorithm to be processed by the machine, making her a pioneer in computer programming.

Frequency Hopping (1940)

Hedy Lamarr pioneered the technology that forms the basis for today's WiFi, GPS, and Bluetooth communication systems. She developed frequency hopping,in which the transmitter and receiver rapidly switch between different channels in a predetermined sequence.

Compiler (1952)

Grace Hopper developed the first compiler called A-0, which translated mathematical code into machine-readable code—an important step toward creating modern programming languages. She also helped create COBOL (a programming language), and standardized the military's computers so that databases could be accessed more easily.


Acorn Micro-Computer (1978)

Sophie Wilson is a British computer scientist who played a key role in the development of the Acorn Micro-Computer, which led to the creation of the ARM (Advanced RISC Machines) processor architecture. ARM processors are widely used in smartphones, tablets, and other electronic devices.

Batteries (1980)

Olga González-Sanabria developed batteries known as long cycle-life nickel-hydrogen batteries while working with NASA. This monumental achievement and was put to use in the International Space Station power system. This type of battery, on average, could run for 40,000 cycles, and last for 10-15 years.

AnitaB.org (1987)

Founded in 1987 by Anita Borg, this focuses on women in technology and creating inclusive environments for women through events and by providing resources, and advocates for diversity and equity in the tech industry.
